Cargo Overview — 2026.04.01

Cargo Overview

Cargo Profile: 765 kV Single-Phase Transformer

Each movement was based on one TM-63 transformer (765 kV single-phase) with a unit weight of 217.0 t and overall dimensions of 5.80 m × 4.20 m × 4.95 m. The operating configuration used a 12-axle SPMT (2 × 6 axle), a 12 m linkspan and 2 support beams, with two SPMT sets deployed in parallel from Trip 1 onward: one remaining at MZP for load-out and beam replacement, and another stationed at AGI for load-in and jack-down. The original loading basis was 2 TR per trip (requiring approximately 4 trips for 7 units), but after critical ballast-calculation errors and excessive linkspan dwell time were identified, Mammoet proposed changing to 1 TR per trip without ballasting on 2026-01-20, and Samsung confirmed the revision. This removed the ballast sequence and extended the programme to 7 individual trips.

Key Lessons and Follow-up Actions
Operational Lessons

Key Lessons and Follow-up Actions

Lessons consistently identified from the original Method Statement, email threads and MOC documents.

L1
Lock In Scope Boundaries at the Early Engineering Stage

If the calculation scope for Ballast, Ramp, Mooring and Stability is defined late, the overall schedule is at risk. Responsibility boundaries must be clearly agreed at the start of the engineering stage.

L2
Linkspan Certification Is a Pre-Load-out Hold Point

Without the Linkspan Certificate, Load Test Evidence, Arrangement Drawing and MOC signed copy (if required), Load-out stops. The submission D-day must be specified in the contract.

L3
Secure the Initial Loading Condition and Tank Arrangement First

Intact Stability Check cannot proceed without the Vessel Initial Condition. Securing the tank plan and ballast basis is the first priority at the engineering stage.

L4
Ballast Pump Capacity Is a Core Design Input

The LCT Bushra 10 t/h ballast pump limitation directly constrains even-keel maintenance and load-out sequence planning. Vessel capacity must be captured as an early engineering input.

L5
Simplify Document Transmission and Revision Control

Documents were repeatedly split and sent in parts due to email size limits, with Google Drive links shared as workarounds.
Without a dedicated voyage document folder and a Rev Freeze policy, confusion will recur.
Actual operations run across Maqta Gateway, ATLP and email in parallel, requiring per-voyage re-issuance of documents.

L6
Freeze the Loading Basis Before HM / PTW Submission

The original 2 TR/trip plan was reduced to 1 TR/trip on 2026-01-20 after three ballast-calculation errors (aft draft below 2.70 m, LCT draft exceeding allowable depth, tide reference roughly double the official tables) and excessive linkspan dwell time for a 217 t cargo at 4.95 m height. The change removed the 9-stage ballast sequence and shifted the programme from an initial 2 TR/trip basis, requiring approximately four trips for seven units, to 7 individual trips.

L7
Define Concurrent SPMT Mobilisation at the Planning Stage

From Trip 1, one set remained at MZP for load-out preparation while a second set with jacking gear was shipped to AGI on LCT Wardeh and LCT JPT62 for offloading and jack-down. This split was essential for cycle time but was not reflected in the initial Method Statement.

L8
Require Independent Peer Review on Ballast and Stability Packages

Earlier draft and tide-reference errors introduced avoidable risk that a single independent check would have caught. Every ballast and stability calculation must pass an internal peer review before submission.

L9
Enforce the Approved Voyage Photo Checklist

Incomplete field photography leads to handover disputes. Every image must carry date, time, location, operation stage and photographer metadata.
